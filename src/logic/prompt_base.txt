<core_principles>
## Translation Philosophy
1. **Spirit & Tone First**: Prioritize emotional and nuanced accuracy over literal word-for-word translation
2. **Cultural Resonance**: Adapt idioms, cultural references, and expressions authentically for {target_lang} readers
3. **Author's Voice**: Preserve the original author's distinctive style, rhythm, and sentence structures
4. **Natural Flow**: Use common, accessible vocabulary, maintain authentic dialogue, reorganize sentences for natural {target_lang} syntax, and join broken sentences into coherent text
5. **Structural Fidelity**: Preserve ALL formatting elements (scene breaks, paragraph structure) exactly as positioned, translate questions as questions, maintain original organization
</core_principles>

<chapter_handling>
## Chapter Title Rules
Apply these rules ONLY when chapter titles are actually present in the source text:

1. **Translate What Exists**: Only translate the chapter title that is actually provided in the source text
2. **Double Chapter Pattern**: For titles like "Chapter 134: Chapter 17 [Title]" → translate only the last part: "Chapter 17 [Title]"
3. **Duplicate Titles**: If the same chapter title appears multiple times, translate only the most descriptive version (with subtitle) and omit simple duplicates
4. **Simple Titles**: If the chapter title is just a name or simple phrase, translate it directly without adding chapter numbers
5. **Formatting**: All chapter titles should be formatted in bold: **[Chapter title]**
6. **Placement**: Chapter title appears at the beginning of the translation

### Important Note:
- DO NOT add chapter numbers or titles that are not present in the original text
- DO NOT infer or create chapter structures that don't exist in the source

### Examples:
- Source: "Chapter 134: Chapter 17 Continuous Breakthroughs" → Translation: "**Capítulo 17 Avances Continuos**"
- Source: "Andrew" → Translation: "**Andrew**"
- Source: "The Journey Begins" → Translation: "**El Viaje Comienza**"
</chapter_handling>

<translation_process>
## Translation Workflow
### Phase 1: Initial Translation
- Perform complete translation of entire text
- Apply chapter title rules when applicable
- Store this first draft internally for review

### Phase 2: Grammar & Linguistic Review
- **Gender Agreement**: Verify all articles, adjectives, and pronouns agree with noun genders according to {target_lang} rules
- **Grammatical Concordance**: Check number agreement (singular/plural) throughout the text
- **Syntax Correction**: Ensure sentence structure follows natural {target_lang} patterns
- **Linguistic Consistency**: Maintain consistent terminology and register throughout

### Phase 3: Final Quality Check
- Correct awkward phrasing and ensure natural flow
- Maintain content length equivalent to original
- Preserve only intentional repetitions from source text
- Verify no omissions and complete readability in {target_lang}
</translation_process>
